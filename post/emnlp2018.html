DAY 1

Julia Hirschberg Keynote: “Truth or Lie? Spoken Indicators of Deception in Speech”
notes
- deceptive speech -- deliberate choice to mislead - criminals are better than police, judges and similar in detecting it - there are acoustic-prosodic and lexical cues - system should take individual differences into account: gender, ethinicity... - existing dataset: Columbia SRI Colorado Deception corpus (7h of speech, 32 subjects, 70% acc) - **created a cross-lingual dataset**: 340 subjects, 122h of speech, native english speakers questioning native chinese speakers who know english (and vice versa) - **features**: n-grams, LIWC, word embeddings, speech-based (openSMILE, ISO9), gender, native language, NEO-FFIs personality scores, syntactic features - Methods: Random Forest, SVM, DL -- BiLSTM-lexical + DNN-speech yielding 0.65 F1 - Best model: Multi-Task Learning with personality traits **74.4 F1** - Significant differences in deceptive speech: Men are loud with higher pich when lying; Woman are just loud, but have jitter when telling the truth - Human accuracy: 56.76%

Session 1: Social Applications 1

Privacy-preserving Neural Representations of Text
Coavoux, Narayan, and Cohen
- explicit private information can be preprocessed, but implicit (age, authorship) can't - **Can attacker guess about input text based on hidden representation?** - Target private variables: age, gender, named entities that occur in the text - attacker eavesdrops while learning the model -- attacker beats the baseline for age and gender just based on hidden r - defence: double objective (loss_clf = -logP(y|x) - loss_attacker) or adversarial generation (LM) - conclusion: latent representations contain a signal for private information - code on [github](https://github.com/mcoavoux/pnet)
Adversarial Removal of Demographic Attributes from Text Data
Elazar and Goldberg
- while learning DeepEmoji attacker eavesdrops --> hich leakage - Actively reduce leakage with adversarial setup (Ganing and Lempitsy, 2015) --> remove stuff from representation, but still some leakage - Their method: use more layers for adversarial reversal layer; bigger \lambda to reverse with; > 1 adversaries --> they learned diff features - still not perfect!
###### DeClarE: Debunking Fake News and False Claims using Evidence-Aware Deep Learning
Popat, Mukherjee, Yates, and Weikum
- prior work: supervised learning (doesn't use external knowledge) & external sources (require feature modeling and lexicons). Why people don't like feature modeling any more? :) - *overcame flaws* with: end-to-end NN that takes the external resources into account without human intervention - Approach: - external evidence -- *search engine* - language style, semantics -- *BiLSTM* - interaction claim-external -- *Attention* - credibility score based on all of that - beat the baseline on 4 datasets - analysis showed clear clusters of Fake and Trusted websites (CNN...)
It’s going to be okay: Measuring Access to Support in Online Communities
Wang and Jurgens
- minimal identity cues are important -- username that reveals gender or interests (catlady22, pizzamaniac...) - support is more than encouragement (14 strategies: validation, advice...) - dataset: post-reply from Reddit, Wiki, Stack Exchange - goal: **rate the reply as**: *support, polite, agree, offensive* - most important features are from LIWC --> support in theory - constructed massive gender-performance DS from usernames - **Who recieves support?** -- woman get more support, but also more disparagement - code and data on [github](https://github.com/davidjurgens/support)

Session 2: Social Media

Joint Learning for Emotion Classification and Emotion Cause Detection
Chen, Hou, Cheng, and Li
- two tasks: (1) emotion classification and (2) emotion cause detection - they argue **it's not hard to label cause while you are labeling for emotions** - joint encoder & linear decoder - joint learning improves F1 (is it significant? --> check paper)
Exploring Optimism and Pessimism in Twitter Using Deep Learning
Caragea, Dinu, and Dumitru
- they argue **there is a difference between sentiment and optimisim/pessimism** - Dataset: 10k tweets labeled between -3 (most pessimistic) to 3 (most optimistic) - when 0 is treshold --> 80% Acc; when (-1) and 1 are --> 90% Acc - deep learning architecture - different from sentiment: when trained on sentiment dataset, they get poor results. Q: They trained only Stanford analyzer, what when you train a NN like for the main task? - verb tense analysis
Predicting News Headline Popularity with Syntactic and Semantic Knowledge Using Multi-Task Learning
Lamprinidis, Hardt, and Hovy
- why do you **click on a Facebook post?** - modeling reader's knowledge -- lexical, syntactic, topical - Dataset: 82k headlines with click numbers. Then make it a binary classification >50% median are often clicked ones, <50% rarely clicked. Q: Seems kinda random. - **Auxiliary task: POS tagging** - GRU-MTL with pretrained embeddings yields better performance than the task alone - Q: Why does POS matter for this? How to know which aux task will help? Also, position of a post, image of a post?
Hybrid Neural Attention for Agreement/Disagreement Inference in Online Debates
Chen, Du, Bing, and Xu
- does a reply agree or disagree with the comment? - two factors: - **self-attention** --> focuses on important parts inside a comment - **cross-comment-attention** --> reply focused - connect both with something, idk what because they show huge NN for 2 sec :( - Attention visualisation & Cross Matrix Product Visualisation --> TODO: Learn how to do that!
Increasing In-Class Similarity by Retrofitting Embeddings with Demographic Information
Hovy and Fornaciari
- Homophily? - retrofitting -- moving the points of distinct classes away from each other (x', y') = a(x, y) + b(mean) - for test data use translation matrix calculated from the train set - benefit in gender and age performance - Q: Not different than just learning the embeddings? If you train your embeddings, you're sepperating classes?

Session 4: Language Models

Efficient Contextualized Representation: Language Model Pruning for Sequence Labeling
Liu et al.
- ELMo is great, but it contains sufficent information --> prune it w.r.t. the task you're solving - dense connectivity -- alows better layer selection via layer-wise dropout - sequence labeling with contextualized representations (PPL?) - nice experiments, **results comparable with SOTA** - **size reduced x10, speed goes up x5-6**, Acc down by just 1% - integrated & pruned plug-and-play model

DAY 2

Session 5: Machine Learning 2

Deriving Machine Attention from Human Rationales
Bao, Chang, Yu, and Barzilay
- they wanna **train NN with less data** - rationales -- part of a sentence that directs sentiment --> use them to direct attention - Rationale2Attention -- transformable from task to task - train R2A on source task - use R2A to generate attention for target task - train a target classifier with R2A attention - **domain-ivariant encoder** --> **Attention Generator** (shared BiLSTM, att.sumarizer) --> **MTL** (shared BiLSTM, task-specific Attention and MLP) - 200 examples + rationales gets the same results as 3100 examples --> **cheaper annotation** - code on [github](https://github.com/YujiaBao/R2A)
Semi-Supervised Sequence Modeling with Cross-View Training
Clark, Luong, Manning, and Le
- revisit self-training (older method) with NN - Blum 1998. idea: 2 models teach each other - alternate labeled and unlabeled batches --> how to use it? - Cross-View Training -- not just exchange predictions like Blum, but use NN shared parameters - 2 models (auxiliary models) + Main Predictor - Forward LSTM --> Forward prediction; Backward LSTM --> Backward prediction - Main Predictor teaches Forward - 4 aux tasks - CVT + MTL on 1B unlabeled data create all labels for all tasks on one example - 4 sequence tagging, dependency parsing, machine translation - significant improvement for CVT + MTL (over ELMo too)
[TACL] Comparing Bayesian Models of Annotation
Paun et al.
- probabilistic models of annotation are better than majority vote and similar

Session 9: Sentiment 1 & Machine Learning 3

Sentiment Classification towards Question-Answering with Hierarchical Matching Network
Shen et al.
- BiLSTM to get encoding of a Question and an Answer - row-wise Answer-to-Question attention & Question-to-Answer - Bidirectional Matching Vectors + self-attention - sentence segmentation helps to extract relevant subtext - Q&A attentions contribute in both ways - dataset on [github](https://github.com/clshenNLP/QASC/)
Cross-topic Argument Mining from Heterogeneous Sources
Stab et al.
- information seeking perspective --> extract pros and cons - apply model to heterogeneous texts (**8 topics, 25k docs**, blogs/news/social media etc.) - Labels: pro, con, no-argument - 200 random sentences for each topic for calibration (0.72 Kappa) --> 25k sentences with crowdsourcing annotation - Bi Contextual LSTM (Gogh et al, 2017) - Tried transfer learning & MTL (topic_main & topic_otherdataset) - train on 7, test on 1 topic - **the more you add, the more you get** - online demo (couldn't find it)
Summarizing Opinions: Aspect Extraction Meets Sentiment Prediction and They Are Both Weekly Supervised
Angelidis and Lapata
- I like the title - extract small number of reviews of a product with different aspects and summarize it (and add sentiment) - not enought data --> inject prior knowledge with seed words - Multi-Seed Aspect Extractor (MATE) -- init seed matrices for each aspect (image, sound of a TV...) - add MTL - Sentiment prediction: multiple instance learning network - dataset: aspect labels, opinion salience labels, find summaries - **TODO**: read the paper
Phrase-level Self-Attention Networks for Universal Sentence Encoding
Wu, Wang, Liu, and Ma
- pre-trained embedding options: - LM -- ELMo, BERT - supervised learning -- their choice --> **smaller model, faster training** - incorporate syntax into fully-attention network -- compose sentence representation on phrased from the dependency tree --> phrase-level self-attention
BanditSum: Extractive Summarization as a Contextual Bandit
Dong et al.
- how to select the right sentence to include in a summary? - Problem: from continuous NN to discrete (N sent, ROUGE) - Sequential Tagging with Reinforcment Learning (proposed before) --> probl: stop sampling once M sentences are selected - Approach: treat extractive summarization as contextual Bandit (Context-Arm-Reward) - smaller search space - no heuristics needed - SOTA

Session 10: Discourse

A Skeleton-Based Model for Promoting Coherence Among Sentences in Narrative Story Generation
Xu et al.
- narative story generation task -- based on a short input, output a story --> requires tight semantic connection - connection among sentences is in **key phrases (skeleton)**: - model the relation among skeletons - **generate a skeleton, then expand to complete a fluent sentence** - Generative module: - input-to-skeleton -- seq2seq - skeleton-to-target sentence -- seq2seq - Skeleton extraction module: - reformulate as a sentence compression problem -- seq2seq - improve the quality with reinforcement learning. What's the reward? - Dataset: Visual storytelling --> based on a first sentence, generate rest - BLEU +44.8% baseline - G-score? Fluency + Coherence
Nexus Network: Connecting the Preceding and the Following in Dialogue Generation
Shen, Su, Li, and Klakow
- user --> NLU --> state tracker --> dialog policy --> NLG --> - nexus: good response should connect the preceding dialogue context and the followup, not just stand-alone - DailyDialog (Li, 2017): 13k conversations, 10 topics - new sota - najgori talk, a napravili brutalne stvari
A Neural Local Coherence Model for Text Quality Assesment
Mesgar and Strube
- local coherence -- relatedness of sentence to the next one - so far: entity-based relations & lexical relations --> connect - Model: - pre-train embeddings - LSTM context info - find max similarity between LSTM features to model topic of 2 proceeding sentences - CNN to extract topic changes - output is a vector that represents coherence - DS: 105 articles, which one is more readable? - SOTA, but not significant compared to DeCLark - Does coherence representation improve Essey Assesment? (ASAP ds on kaggle) --> yes, on esseys that are scored by humans with criteria "Must be logical sequence of events" (narrative prompts) - Q: Why only take one word from each sentence with LSTM?
Deep Attentive Sentence Ordering Network
Cui, Li, Chen, and Zhang
- sentence ordering task -- o = [4, 1, 3, 2]; o' = [1, 2, 3, 4] - RNN models as baseline (previous work) - propose self-attention paragraph encoder - sentence encoder BiLSTM --> scaled dot product self-attention --> pooling --> decoder --> softmax as coherence probability - use random permutation on training - 7 different datasets, 3 general topics - evaluate tau, accuracy, perfect match ration - tons of experiments. SOTA. - better in longer texts
Getting to “Hearer-old”: Charting Referring Expressions Across Time
Staliunaite, Rohde, Webber, and Louis
- corpus of reffering expressions (Putin, current presindent of Russia) --> When does it stop being neccessary? - Reffering expression = name + descriptor (becomes shorter over time, <=1 words --> hearer-old) - extracting: NP + NP in syntactic tree - 52k entities, 200k+ mentions - How long until it becomes hearer-old? Outperforms the MCC baseline

Modeling Empathy and Distress in Reaction to News Stories
Buechel et al.
- prior work -- no gold, no psychological background - 2 types: - Empathic concern -- positive, other-focused - Personal distress -- negative, self-focused - annotation as psychological experiment -- read article, then write text and label (1-7) for both types - baseline r=0.4 in Empathic and Distress - Best is CNN with FastText

Barbieri et al.
